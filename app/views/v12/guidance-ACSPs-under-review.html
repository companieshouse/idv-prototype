Steps to apply to register as an authorised agent

How to register your business as an authorised agent to use Companies House services.

Contents

  2. 2. Verify your identity
  3. 3. Check the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) registration details
  4. 4. Give details about the business
  5. 5. After you've registered
  6. 6. Comply with your legal responsibilities

Business who file with Companies House on behalf of clients, such as solicitors, accountants or company formation agents, need to register with Companies House to become authorised agents. Authorised agents are also known as authorised corporate service providers (ACSPs).

In the future, businesses will need to be registered as an authorised agent to do any of the following:

  • file on behalf of clients
  • verify their clients' identity for Companies House

1. Check you can register the business

To register your business as a Companies House authorised agent, you must be one of the following:

  • director
  • general partner
  • partner
  • member
  • sole trader
If you’re registering an unincorporated body or corporate body

Unincorporated body

If the body is managed by its members, you must be a member of the body. Otherwise, you must be a member of the governing body.

Corporate body

If the body is managed by its members, you must be a member of the body. Otherwise, you must be the equivalent of a director.

2. Verify your identity

You’ll need to verify your identity for Companies House.

You must do this before you can use the online service to register your business as an authorised agent.

3. Check the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) registration details

The business must have been registered with an AML supervisory body for at least 2 weeks before you apply.

You'll need to tell us the:

  • names of the supervisory bodies the business is registered with
  • AML membership numbers or IDs
Help to find your membership number

Depending on your supervisory body, you may be able to find this on:

  • your practising certificate
  • communications from the supervisory body
  • the supervisory body's online register

Your AML supervisory body may have a different name for it, such as:

  • a firm ID or number
  • a regulation ID
  • an AML ID

You should contact the AML supervisory body if you need to check your details.

We'll confirm the supervisory body has the same name and membership number for your business. If the details do not match, we'll reject your application.

The next section will explain which name we'll confirm with the supervisory body for your business.

4. Give details about your business

The details you need to give will depend on the type of business you’re registering.

Limited companies, limited partnerships (LPs) and limited liability partnerships (LLPs)

You'll need to tell us:

  • the company number
  • company authentication code
  • which business sector your work in

We’ll check your AML supervisory body holds the company name that’s shown on the Companies House register. If the AML registration is in a different name, you’ll need to update this before you apply.

Sole traders

This means you are self-employed and registered with HMRC for Self Assessment.

You'll need to tell us:

  • your name
  • your date of birth
  • your nationality
  • the country you live in
  • your correspondence address (this must be in the UK)

You'll also need to tell us if your supervisory body holds your name, your business name, or both.

Partnerships and other unincorporated entities (not including sole traders)

You'll need to tell us the:

  • business name
  • business address (this must be in the UK)
  • correspondence address

If you are registered with the AML supervisory body with your own name, you'll also need to give your name. Otherwise, we’ll check the AML supervisory body holds the business name.

5. After you've registered

If your application is accepted, we'll register your business as a Companies House authorised agent.

We’ll create an authorised agent account for the business. You’ll be able to access this when you sign in to your Companies House account.

Add users to the agent account

If other people who work for the business need to file with Companies House, you can add them to the account.

You can choose if you want them to be able to:

  • verify people's identity for Companies House
  • make changes to the authorised agent's details
  • add or remove other users

What we'll show on the public list of authorised agents

We'll show your agent registration status. For example, if you are an active agent, or if you have been suspended or stopped from acting as an authorised agent.

We’ll also show the:

  • date you registered as an authorised agent
  • name of your business, or you own name if you’re a sole trader and do not have a different business name
  • the town or city of your business address, or your correspondence address if you’re a sole trader
  • the names of all AML supervisory bodies your business is registered with

6. Comply with your legal responsibilities

You must tell us if there is a change to any of the information you provided when you registered as an authorised agent. If there are changes to your AML registration status or details, you must tell us within 14 days.

You must provide more information if we ask for it. This could be about:

  • your filings with Companies House
  • identity checks, if you have verified someone’s identity for Companies House

If you do verify people’s identity for Companies House, you’ll need to keep records of any checks you complete for 7 years.

! Warning We could suspend or stop your business from acting as an authorised agent if you do not comply with your responsibilities. You could also get a fine or face criminal prosecution.
