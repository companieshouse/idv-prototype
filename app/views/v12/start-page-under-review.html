Use this service to register your business as an authorised agent with Companies House. Authorised agents are also known as Authorised Corporate Service Providers (ACSPs).

In the future, businesses such as solicitors, accountants or company formation agents, will need to register as a Companies House authorised agent to do any of the following:

  • file on behalf of clients
  • verify people's identity for Companies House

Who can apply

To register your business as an authorised agent, you must be one of the following:

  • director
  • general partner
  • member
  • sole trader
If you’re registering an unincorporated body or corporate body

Unincorporated body

If the body is managed by its members, you must be a member of the body. Otherwise, you must be a member of the governing body.

Corporate body

If the body is managed by its members, you must be a member of the body. Otherwise, you must be the equivalent of a director.

If other people who work for the business need to file with Companies House, you can add them to the authorised agent account once you've registered.

Verify your identity

You’ll need to verify your identity for Companies House before you can use this service.

Verify your identity for Companies House.

What information you need to submit

You’ll need to:

  • give details about the business, or yourself if you’re a sole trader
  • tell us which Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory bodies the business is registered with
  • give the AML membership numbers or IDs
  • pay the £XX registration fee

Read the guidance on applying to register as an authorised agent to check you have everything you need to apply.

This service will time out if you do not use it for 60 minutes. You can save your answers and resume your application later.
