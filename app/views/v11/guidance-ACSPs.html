Guidance

Applying to register as an authorised agent

What this service is for

Use this service to register your business as an authorised agent with Companies House. Authorised agents are also known as Authorised Corporate Service Providers (ACSPs).

For now, you only need to register as an authorised agent if you want to verify the identity of your clients for Companies House.

From XX DATE, individuals or organisations will need to register as a Companies House authorised agent to do any of the following:

  • file on behalf of clients
  • form companies or registrable partnerships on behalf of clients
  • verify someone's identity for Companies House

Who can use this service

To register as an authorised agent, your business must be registered:

  • in the UK
  • with an Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body - you must have been registered for at least 2 weeks

To use this service you must be one of the following:

  • director
  • general partner
  • partner
  • sole trader

What information you need to submit

You must have been registered with an AML supervisory body for at least 2 weeks before you apply to register as an authorised agent. You will need to tell us the:

  • names of all AML supervisory bodies the business is registered with
  • AML membership numbers or IDs
Where to find your AML membership number

Depending on your supervisory body, you may be able to find this on: 

  • your practising certificate
  • communications from the supervisory body
  • the supervisory body's online register

It may also be known as:

  • a firm ID or number
  • a member ID or number
  • a regulation ID
  • an AML ID
We'll confirm the AML supervisory body has the same name and membership number for your business. Contact your AML supervisory body if you need to check your details before you apply.

If your business is registered with Companies House

This includes limited companies, limited partnerships (LPs) and limited liability partnerships (LLPs).

You will need give the:

  • business name - this must be the same as the name the AML supervisory body holds
  • registered office address
  • company number
  • company authentication code

If you are a sole trader

This means you are self-employed and registered with HMRC for Self Assessment.

You'll need to verify your identity for Companies House before you can apply to register as an authorised agent.

To register as an authorised agent, you'll need to give:

  • your name
  • your date of birth
  • your nationality
  • the country you live in
  • your correspondence address (this must be in the UK)

If you are a partnership or another unincorporated business (not including sole traders)

This includes partnerships (that are not registered with Companies House) and other unincorporated entities.

You will need to give the:

  • business name
  • business address (this must be in the UK)
  • correspondence address

If you are registered with the AML supervisory body with your own name, you'll also need to give your name.

What we'll show on the public list of authorised agents

We publish a list of all registered Companies House authorised agents. This will show your registration status - for example if you are an active agent, or if you have been suspended or deauthorised.

We’ll also show the:

  • date you registered as an authorised agent
  • name of your business, or you own name if you’re a sole trader and do not have a different business name
  • the town or city of your business address, or your correspondence address if you’re a sole trader
  • the name of all AML supervisory bodies your business is registered with

What happens after the authorised agent is registered

You'll be able to sign in to your Companies House account to access your authorised agent account.

Adding or removing people who work for the authorised agent

You can add people who work for the business as members to the authorised agent account. You can choose if you want members to be able to:

  • verify someone’s identity
  • make changes to the authorised agent's details
  • add or remove other members

What you'll need to do as an authorised agent

Once registered, you or another member of the authorised agent must:

  • tell us if there is a change to your Anti-Money Laundering (AML) registration status, or the supervisory authority details you have provided – you must do this within 14 days
  • provide more information to Companies House if requested – this could be about your business, or records of your filings with Companies House
  • keep records of any evidence you use to verify someone’s identity for 7 years, if you verify the identity of clients for Companies House
! Warning Your business may be suspended or deauthorised from acting as a Companies House authorised agent if you do not comply with the requirements. You could also get a fine or face criminal prosecution.

Verifying someone's identity for Companies House

Authorised agents do not have to offer identity verification to their clients. Agents that verify their clients identity for Companies House will need to meet the identity verification standard.

Read the guidance about verifying someone's identity for Companies House for information on how you can meet the standard.
