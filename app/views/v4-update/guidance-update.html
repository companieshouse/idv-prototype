Update an authorised agents details with Companies House

Guidance for authorised agents who want to verify people's identity for Companies House.

What this service is for

This service is for Companies House authorised agents to tell us when they have verified someone’s identity.

The checks you do to verify someone’s identity must meet Companies House identity verification standard. You must read the standard to make sure your identity checks reach the required level of assurance before you use this service.

Who can use this service

You, or the business you work for, must register as a Companies House authorised agent before you can use this service.

What you’ll need to tell us you have verified someone’s identity

When you have completed checks to verify someone’s identity, you will need to use this service to tell us the:

  • person’s name
  • person’s date of birth
  • person’s home address
  • date you completed the identity checks
  • documents you used as evidence to verify their identity

You’ll also need to complete a declaration to confirm that the person is who they claim to be.

If your checks meet the Companies House standard of identity verification, we will send the person their Companies House personal code.

How you can meet the identity verification standard

You can use the following steps to meet the Companies House identity verification standard when you check someone’s identity.

These are guidance and you can choose to verify someone’s identity another way. However, you must make sure the checks you do meet the identity verification standard.

    Ask for information about the person

    Get evidence to verify the person’s identity information

    Check the personal information belongs to the person claiming it

    Check the evidence is real

    Check the person’s identity has existed over time

Other ways to verify someone’s identity

You can use a digital identity platform or commercial provider to verify someone’s identity.

If you do this, you must:

  • ensure the checks are carried out correctly and meet Companies House identity verification standard
  • keep copies of documents and any other evidence

What to do if you cannot verify someone’s identity

The person will need to get documents to be able to verify their identity for Companies House.

They will need to verify their identity to do any of the following:

  • file with Companies House
  • be a director
  • be a Person of Significant Control (PSC)
  • apply to register as a Companies House authorised agent
