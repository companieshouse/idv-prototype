How to register as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP)

Content goes here.

What an ACSP is

ACSPs are intermediaries such as accountants, legal advisers, or company formation agents, that are registered with a supervisory body for Anti-Money Laundering (AML) purposes.

ACSPs must register with Companies House to:

  • verify the identity of individuals for the Companies House register
  • form companies or registrable partnerships, or to file on their behalf

Who can use this service

You must be the person who is responsible for Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance within the business to apply to register the ACSP.

To register as an ACSP, a business must:

  • be registered with an AML supervisory body
  • already carry out customer due diligence checks on all clients

What information you need to submit

If you have not verified your own identity with a GOV.UK account, you will be asked to do this before you register an ACSP. To do this, you’ll need:

  • to create or sign in to a GOV.UK account
  • a relevant photo ID
  • your current home address (you might also need your previous address if you’ve recently moved)

To register the ACSP, you'll need:

  • the name of the company, or your own name if you are self-employed
  • the registered address
  • the name of the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body
  • the AML registration number
  • to pay the £XX registration fee using a credit or debit card

What happens after an ACSP is registered

Once registered, you must provide details about any changes to the ACSP’s AML registration. This includes if:

  • the ACSP is no longer registered with an AML supervisory body
  • the AML registration details change, for example if the ACSP registers with a different AML supervisory body
