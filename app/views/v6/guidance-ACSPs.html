Guidance

Applying to register as an authorised agent

Contents

  1. What this service is for
  2. Who can use this service
  3. What information you need to submit
  4. What happens after an authorised agent is registered

What this service is for

Use this service to register your business as an authorised agent with Companies House. Authorised agents are also known as Authorised Corporate Service Providers (ACSPs).

Agents must register with Companies House to:

  • form companies or registrable partnerships on behalf of clients
  • file on behalf of clients
  • verify someone's identity for Companies House

Who can use this service

To register as an authorised agent, your business must be:

  • registered in the UK
  • registered with an anti-money laundering (AML) supervisory body

You'll need to be a relevant officer for the business to use this service. This means you are one of the following:

  • director
  • general partner
  • partner
  • sole trader

What information you need to submit

You will need to tell us the:

  • names of all AML supervisory bodies the business is registered with
  • AML membership numbers
We will check the AML supervisory body has the same name and membership number for your business. If you are registered personally, you will need to apply as a sole trader using your own details.

If your business is registered with Companies House

This includes limited companies, limited partnerships and limited liability partnerships (LLPs).

You will need the:

  • business name
  • registered office address
  • company number
  • company authentication code

If your business is not registered with Companies House

This includes partnerships and other firms that are not incorporated.

You will need to give your:

  • name
  • date of birth
  • nationality

You will also need to give the:

  • business name
  • business address (this must be in the UK)
  • correspondence address

If you are a sole trader

You'll need to verify your identity for Companies House (opens in a new tab) before you can apply to register as an authorised agent.

To register as an authorised agent, you'll need to give:

  • your name
  • your date of birth
  • your nationality
  • the state or county where you live
  • your correspondence address (this must be in the UK)

What happens after the authorised agent is registered

You'll be able to sign in to your Companies House account to access online services for authorised agents.

We’ll email you the authorised agent number. You should keep the number secure and must not share it with anyone outside of your organisation. You will need to use it:

  • for paper filings
  • to update the authorised agent’s details

Add or remove members who work for the authorised agent

Members can sign in or create a Companies House account to:

  • form companies and registrable partnerships on behalf of clients
  • file on behalf of clients

You can choose if you want members to be able to:

  • verify someone's identity
  • add other members
  • change the authorised agent details, for example the AML supervisory body

What you'll need to do as an authorised agent

You must provide details about any changes to the agent's AML registration. This includes if the:

  • agent is no longer registered with an AML supervisory body
  • AML registration details change, for example if the agent registers with a different AML supervisory body

Identity verification checks

Authorised agents do not have to offer identity verification to their clients. However, they will still need to register to file on behalf of companies or registrable partnerships.

Read the guidance about verifying someone's identity for Companies House.
