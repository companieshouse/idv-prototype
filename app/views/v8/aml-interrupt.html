Your AML supervisory body must hold the same name as your application

To confirm your Anti-Money Laundering (AML) registration, we need to check the supervisory body holds the same name and membership number as you give on your application.

If you are a soler trader, you will need to be registered with your own name. Otherwise, you will need to be registered as a business with the AML supervisory body.

What to do next

If you’re registering a company, partnership or another type of organisation, you’ll need toregister the business with an AML supervisory body first.

Or, to register yourself as an authorised agent, you can apply as a sole trader. Your AML registration will need to be in your own name.
