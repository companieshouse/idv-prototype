Use this service to register your business as Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP) with Companies House. ACSPs are also known as Companies House authorised agents.

You'll need to register to be able to:

  • verify your clients' identities for Companies House
  • file on behalf of clients

Who can apply

To register your business as an authorised agent, you must be one of the following:

  • director
  • general partner
  • partner
  • member
  • sole trader
If you’re registering an unincorporated body or corporate body

Corporate body

If the body is managed by its members, you must be a member of the body. Otherwise, you must be the equivalent of a director.

Unincorporated entity

If the body is managed by its members, you must be a member of the body. Otherwise, you must be a member of the governing body.

You’ll be able to add other people who work for the business to the authorised agent account once you’ve registered.

How to apply

You'll need to do the following steps to complete your application. You can save your answers and resume your application later if you need to.

Sign in and verify your identity

You'll need to sign in to, or create a GOV.UK One Login to use this service.

If you have not already verified your identity for Companies House with the email address you use for GOV.UK One Login, you’ll need to either:

  • verify your identity – read the guidance on verifying to check what you'll need
  • provide your Companies House personal code

You’ll use the same email address to access the authorised agent account once you have registered. You will be able to change this, if you want to.

Tell us information about the business, or yourself if you're a sole trader

The information you need to provide will depend on the type of business you're registering.

Read the guidance on applying to register as an authorised agent to check you have everything you need.

Provide Anti-Money Laundering (AML) registration details

You’ll need to tell us:

  • which AML supervisory bodies the business is registered
  • AML membership numbers or IDs - your supervisory body may have a different name for this, such as a firm ID or number

We’ll check the supervisory body holds the same name and membership number for your business. Contact your AML supervisory body if you need to check your details.

Pay the £55 registration fee

You'll need to pay using a credit or debit card. We'll refund the fee if the application is rejected.
