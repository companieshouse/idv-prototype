Apply to register as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP)

Use this service to register yourself or your business as an ACSP. This is also known as an authorised agent.

For now, you only need to register to verify people's identities for Companies House.

Who can apply

To register your business, you must be one of the following:

  • director
  • general partner
  • partner
  • member
  • sole trader
  • member of the body – if you're registering an unincorporated body or corporate body

You’ll be able to add other people who work for the business to the authorised agent account once you’ve registered.

What you'll need

You'll need to sign in to or create a GOV.UK One Login to use this service.

If you haven't already verified your identity, we'll ask you to do this when you start. Read the guidance on verifying your identity for Companies House to check what you’ll need to do this.

To apply, you'll need to:

  • tell us about the business, or yourself if you’re a sole trader
  • tell us which Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory bodies the business is supervised by
  • provide the AML membership numbers, sometimes known as your practice number or firm ID
  • pay the £55 registration fee using a credit or debit card
! Warning We'll reject your application if the AML details you provide are incorrect. Contact your supervisory body if you need to check these details.

Read the guidance on applying to register as an authorised agent to check you have everything you need to apply.

You can save your answers and resume your application later if you need to.

Read the guidance on being an ACSP to find out more about what happens after you register, including your legal responsibilities.
