How to register an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP)

Contents

  1. What an ACSP is
  2. Who can use this service
  3. What information you need to submit
  4. What happens after an ACSP is registered

What an ACSP is

An ACSP is an agent such as an accountant, legal adviser, or company formation agent, that is registered with a supervisory body for Anti-Money Laundering (AML) purposes.

These businesses must register as an ACSP with Companies House to:

  • verify the identity of individuals for the Companies House register
  • form companies or registrable partnerships, or to file on their behalf

Who can use this service

You must be a relevant officer to register an ACSP. This means that you are responsible for AML compliance within the business.

To register as an ACSP, a business must:

  • be registered with an AML supervisory body
  • already carry out customer due diligence checks on all clients

What information you need to submit

If you have not verified your own identity with a GOV.UK account, you will be asked to do this before you can register an ACSP.

To do this, you’ll need:

  • to create or sign in to a GOV.UK account
  • a relevant photo ID
  • your current home address (you might also need your previous address if you’ve recently moved)

To register the ACSP, you'll need:

  • the name of the company, or your own name if you are self-employed
  • the registered address
  • the name of the AML supervisory body
  • the AML registration number
  • to pay the £XX registration fee using a credit or debit card

What happens after an ACSP is registered

You must provide details about any changes to the ACSP’s AML registration. This includes if the:

  • ACSP is no longer registered with an AML supervisory body
  • AML registration details change, for example if the ACSP registers with a different AML supervisory body

If you are no longer the person who is responsible for AML compliance, you must give details for a new relevant officer.
