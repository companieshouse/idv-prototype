Guidance

Filing with Companies House on behalf of clients

Agents filing on behalf of clients will need to register as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP). This is also known as a Companies House authorised agent.

Who can file with Companies House on behalf of clients

From XX date, only agents that have registered as an Authorised Corporate Service Provider (ACSP), can file with Companies House on behalf of clients. ACSPs are also known as Companies House authorised agents.

This is a new legal requirement that will help us to know who's filing information on the public register.

To be able to register, agents must be supervised by a UK Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body. For example, this could include:

  • company formation agents
  • solicitors
  • accountants

An agent can be a business (for example, a limited company or partnership), or an individual who files on behalf of others (sole trader).

Who needs to register as an authorised agent

For now, only agents that intend to verify the identity of clients for Companies House need to register.

From spring 2026, agents that file any other information on behalf of clients will also need to register.

To apply to register, you must have a senior role in the business. For example, you must be a director if you’re registering a limited company, a general partner if you're registering a limited partnership (LP), or a member of the partnership if you're registering a partnership or Limited Liability Partnership (LLP).

Sole traders can register themselves as an authorised agent.

Once a business is registered, we'll create an online account for the authorised agent. You’ll be able to add other people who work for the agent to the account. This will allow them to use services for the authorised agent.

Read the guidance on applying to register as an authorised agent to check if you can apply.

How to use your authorised agent account

You’ll be able to access the authorised agent account when you sign in to your Companies House account.

Add and remove users

You should add people who work for the business if they need to use Companies House services. They will not need to verify their identity.

You can manage what users can do by selecting their role when you add them to the account. There are 3 roles users can have for an authorised agent:

  • account owners – can update the authorised agent's details, and add and remove all users
  • administrators – can add and remove administrators and standard users
  • standard users – cannot add and remove other users

You can remove users from the account at any time. Once you remove a user, they will no longer be able to access the account or services for the authorised agent.

Update the authorised agent's details

An account owner must:

  • update the details on the authorised agent account if there are any changes to the information we hold about the agent – they must do this within 14 days
  • close the authorised agent account if the agent is no longer registered with an AML supervisory body

Legal requirements for authorised agents

Companies House authorised agents must comply with the following legal requirements.

  1. The agent must always be registered with at least one Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body.
  2. An account owner must tell us if there is a change to any of the information we hold about the agent. They must do this within 14 days.
  3. If we request it, the agent must provide more information:
    • about the agent's filings with Companies House
    • about identity checks, if the agent verifies someone’s identity for Companies House
    • to prove the agent is complying with its legal responsibilities
  4. The agent must keep records of any identity checks for 7 years, if it has verified someone's identity for Companies House.
! Warning We may suspend or stop authorised agents that do not comply with these requirements. Anyone who has a senior role for the agent, such as directors, could receive a fine or face criminal prosecution.

What happens if we need to suspend or stop an authorised agent

We will suspend or stop an authorised agent if it does not comply with its legal responsibilities.

If we need to suspend an authorised agent

We'll send an email to the agent's correspondence email address with information about what to do next.

We may need to ask the agent to provide more information or records about their filings. The agent will have 28 days to do this.

Users that have been added to the authorised agent account will not be able to access services for the authorised agent. This means they will not be able to file on behalf of the agent while it is suspended.

People who have had their identity verified by the agent for Companies House may need to verify their identity again. We will contact anyone who needs to do this.

We’ll also publish information to show that the authorised agent has been suspended. We’ll publish the agent’s name, status and the date it was suspended.

If we need to stop an authorised agent

We will:

  • send an email to the agent’s correspondence email address
  • close the online account – users will not be able to access the agent account or services
  • publish the agent’s name, status and the date it was disqualified
  • contact anyone whose identity the agent has verified, if they need to verify again

Services for authorised agents

All users that have been added to an authorised agent account will be able to access services for authorised agents when they sign in to their Companies House account.

Tell Companies House you've verified someone's identity

From XX DATE, you can use this service to tell us when you've verified someone's identity for Companies House.

If you use this service, you must keep records of the identity checks you do for 7 years.

File as an authorised agent

From XX DATE, you'll be able to file on behalf of clients as an authorised agent.
