What this service is for

Use this service to apply to register as an authorised agent with Companies House. Authorised agents are also known as Authorised Corporate Service Providers (ACSPs).

Agents must register with Companies House to:

  • form companies or registrable partnerships, or to file on their behalf
  • verify the identity of individuals for the Companies House register

What an authorised agent is

An authorised agent can be an agent, such as an accountant, legal adviser, or company formation agent.

To register as an authorised agent with Companies House, they must:

  • be registered in the UK
  • be registered with an Anti-Money Laundering (AML) supervisory body
  • already carry out customer due diligence checks on all clients

Who can use this service

You must be a relevant officer to register an authorised agent. This means that you are responsible for AML compliance within the business. You will be the main contact for the authorised agent.

If the business you’re registering is an organisation, the application will be for the whole organisation. You will be able to add or remove people who work for the agent once it is registered.

What information you need to submit

If you have not proved your own identity with a GOV.UK One Login account, you will be asked to do this before you can register an authorised agent.

Information to prove your identity

You’ll need:

  • to create or sign in to a GOV.UK account
  • a relevant photo ID
  • your current home address (you might also need your previous address if you’ve recently moved)

We’ll only use this information to check that you are who you say you are. We will not show this information on the Companies House register.

Information about the business

You'll need:

  • the name of the company, or your own name if you are self-employed
  • the UK registered address
  • the name of the AML supervisory body
  • the AML registration number
  • to pay the £XX registration fee using a credit or debit card

The company name and registered office address must be exactly the same as the details held by the AML supervisory body.

If you’re registering a limited company, limited liability partnership (LLP), limited partnership or Scottish limited partnership (SLP), you'll also need:

  • the company number
  • the company authentication code

What happens after the authorised agent is registered

You must provide details about any changes to the agent's AML registration. This includes if the:

  • agent is no longer registered with an AML supervisory body
  • AML registration details change, for example if the agent registers with a different AML supervisory body

The authorised agent must give details for a new relevant officer if you are no longer the person who is responsible for AML compliance.

Identity verification checks

Authorised agents do not have to offer identity verification to their clients. However, they will still need to register to file on behalf of companies or registrable partnerships.

If you verify someone’s identity with Companies House, you must keep a copy of any documents you use to check a person’s identity. You'll need to keep these while you have a working relationship and for 5 years after.

We may ask for more information on identity checks if we need to.
